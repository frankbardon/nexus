# Loom Systems

## Overview
Loom Systems is a vertically focused player in the agent-orchestration space,
specializing in financial services workflows (KYC, trade reconciliation, claims
adjudication). Bootstrapped, profitable since 2024, ~80 employees. NYC-based.

## Positioning
Loom doesn't compete on platform breadth; they win on depth in finance. Their
pitch is "we built the compliance and integration work other vendors charge
extra for, and we did it for your industry specifically." Sells almost
exclusively to mid-market banks, asset managers, and insurance carriers.

## Product Surface
- Loom Workbench — domain-specific agent builder with finance primitives baked in.
- Loom Adapters — pre-built connectors to Bloomberg, Refinitiv, Plaid, FIS, Fiserv.
- Loom Audit — SEC/FINRA-flavored audit trail with retention policies pre-configured.
- Loom Sandbox — synthetic-data testing environment for compliance teams.

## Pricing
- Pricing scales with assets under management or transactions processed.
- Typical mid-market deal lands at $120-250k/year.
- No self-serve; all sales are direct.

## Strengths
- Domain expertise; their PMs are ex-Goldman, ex-State Street.
- Pre-built adapters save ~6 months of integration work for finance buyers.
- Profitable + bootstrapped — credible "we'll be here in 10 years" story.
- Audit trail is genuinely better than horizontal competitors.

## Weaknesses
- Verticalization is a liability outside finance — irrelevant to most buyers.
- No open-source story; closed ecosystem.
- Limited tooling for general-purpose AI workflows; you can't easily build
  non-finance agents on top.
- Smaller R&D budget than the VC-funded competitors; ships fewer features per quarter.

## Recent Activity

- 2026-04: Hosted first user conference (LoomCon) in Manhattan; ~400 attendees.
