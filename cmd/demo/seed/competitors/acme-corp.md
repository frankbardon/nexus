# ACME Corp

## Overview
ACME Corp is the incumbent in the agent-orchestration platform space, founded
in 2018 and headquartered in Mountain View, CA. They've raised $480M across
five rounds, most recently a $200M Series E at a $4.2B valuation in late 2025.

## Positioning
ACME positions itself as the "enterprise default" for AI agent workflows. The
pitch leans hard on compliance (SOC2 Type II, HIPAA, FedRAMP Moderate
in-process), single-tenant deployments, and a 5-year LTS commitment on every
major release. Target buyer is a CIO at a Fortune 1000 company, not a developer.

## Product Surface
- ACME Studio — visual workflow builder, drag-and-drop agent composition.
- ACME Runtime — managed multi-cloud execution with autoscaling and observability.
- ACME Catalog — curated marketplace of pre-built agents and tools.
- ACME Compliance — audit-trail-and-policy layer required for regulated workloads.

## Pricing
Quoted publicly at $80k/year minimum for the Enterprise SKU; we've heard from
two prospects that real deals start at $150k after the required Compliance
add-on. No self-serve tier. Free 14-day evaluation requires sales contact.

## Strengths
- Deep enterprise integrations (SAP, Workday, ServiceNow connectors maintained in-house).
- Strongest compliance story in the segment.
- Well-staffed customer success team (1:5 CSM-to-account ratio per their last 10-K).

## Weaknesses
- Closed source; no portability if you want to leave.
- Visual builder is slow to iterate on for power users — most enterprise teams
  end up writing escape-hatch Python anyway.
- Pricing locks out everyone below the $50M revenue range.
- Roadmap moves at quarterly cadence, slow vs the AI tooling space.

## Recent Activity
- 2026-01: Announced FedRAMP Moderate status (in-process, not yet ATO).
- 2026-02: Acquired Beacon Compliance for $35M (audit-trail tech).
- 2026-03: Layoffs of ~8% in engineering, primarily in the Studio team.
